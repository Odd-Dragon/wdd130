Welcome to the Quixotic-Dragons Homebrew directory, where we aim to make navigating player options easy and aesthetically pleasing. For now, this is a test site, and as such, only has the Warlock class information available.

Classes

Tech fanatics and potioncrafters alike, Artificers use intelligence and ingenuity to accomplish their goals.

Fueled by rage, Barbarians are hard to hit, harder to avoid, and easy to play. They're a very good beginner option.

Believe it or not, Bards do not have to be the sluts of the party. They're an excellent support class, with a mix of magic and charisma.

Though they get their power from their faith, Clerics are more than just the stereotypical cloth-wearing priests. They can even wear most types of armor!

Ever wanted to turn into a bear? Now you can with the druid. They're skilled spellcasters and very in touch with their wild sides.

The only homebrew class of the bunch, the Factotum is ported from 3.5 and is essentially a professional adventurer.

No real surprises here, Fighters, well, fight. They get all armor and weapons.

No armor, no weapons, but they still kick all the butt- literally! Monks are unarmed, unarmored powerhouses and skill monkeys.

The love-child of clerics and fighters, Paladins are heavily-armored devotees of certain creeds- though they're not always religous.

Much like paladins are crosses between clerics and fighters, Rangers are a mix of fighter and druid.

Sneaky and nimble, Rogues can stab or steal with equal ease.

Sorcerers are born with their power, and usually proud of it! They're the most versatile spellcaster by far.

Warlocks buy their power from an arcane patron. They've got the most "mix-and-match" build.

Wizards had to work for every spell they know, and they know more than most.
